Dr. Albert Einstein

German-American physicist

Dr. Einstein's Timeline

  • 1884-1889: Einstein attends elementary school in Munich, Germany.
  • 1896-1900: He graduates from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, Switzerland, and becomes a Swiss citizen.
  • 1902: Einstein works as a patent examiner at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.
  • 1905: "Annus Mirabilis" - Einstein publishes four groundbreaking papers that revolutionize physics, including the theory of special relativity and the photoelectric effect.
  • 1908: Einstein becomes a lecturer at the University of Bern.
  • 1909: He is appointed as an associate professor at the University of Zurich.
  • 1909: Einstein becomes a full professor at the German-speaking University of Prague.
  • 1915: Einstein completes the theory of general relativity, which explains gravity as the curvature of spacetime.
  • 1919: General relativity is confirmed during a solar eclipse, propelling Einstein to international fame.
  • 1921: Einstein receives the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.
  • 1933: Due to the rise of the Nazi regime, Einstein emigrates to the United States, where he joins the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.
  • 1939: Einstein signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning of the potential for atomic weapons and advocating for nuclear research, which contributes to the Manhattan Project.
  • 1952: He is offered the presidency of Israel but declines the honor.
  • April 18, 1955: Albert Einstein passes away in Princeton, New Jersey, USA, at the age of 76.
